### Priming

I may have found the solution: priming. Thinking about something and imagining myself doing it will make it much easier to start doing it. If I think about some exciting things, it may stop me from wasting time on games and videos. What makes this executable is that starting to think is painless, unlike exercise, which may help improve self-discipline but requires self-discipline itself. Keep a to-do structure in mind for constant priming.

### ToDo Structure


There are 4 kinds of ToDos: fixed (need to be done at a fixed time, like a meeting), urgent (should be done as soon as possible), float (can float within a week or two, but should allocate enough time for it, like working out, washing clothes, and maintaining intimate relationships), and free.

**Mapping to Obsidian's Tasks Plugin:**
- **Urgent:** Assign a `Due` date (strict deadline).
- **Fixed:** Assign a `Scheduled` date (planned execution).
- **Float:** Assign both `Scheduled` (planned start) and `Due` (latest acceptable completion) to allow flexibility.
- **Free:** No dates assigned (kept in a backlog).

Free is the most important category in the long run. It really distinguishes one from others. In general, it should be allocated towards long-term goals.
1. One principle is, for everything you do, allocate free time to evaluate and improve it. Now in the era of LLMs, there is another thing: to automate it.
2. Gathering information is another typical way of finding new free activities.

## Seek first to understand, then to be understood

When we disagree or feel offended, carefully verify what they mean. Even when the meaning is literally clear, the speaker might not actually mean it. Do not react.
