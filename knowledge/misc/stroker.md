- **Phenoxyethanol (苯氧乙醇)** — preservative

**Source B — [Taobao reseller listing][^tb]** (2025 marketing/种草 page, possibly AI-generated):

The two are mutually inconsistent (PEG-90M vs sodium hyaluronate as the primary thickener), so confidence in either is low. The cosdna entry is more chemically detailed and matches the typical Chinese OTC formulation pattern, but is community-edited and may be for a different SKU. The Taobao copy is reseller marketing.

## Toysheart (对子哈特) water-based lube
Bottle Henry saw — 5 ingredients total: **sodium polyacrylate (聚丙烯酸钠) + glycerin (甘油) + methylparaben (羟苯甲酯 / 对羟基苯甲酸甲酯) + water (水) + EDTA disodium (乙二胺四乙酸二钠)**. Extremely minimal: one slipperiness polymer + one humectant + one preservative + one chelator + water.

The slipperiness driver is **sodium polyacrylate (聚丙烯酸钠)** — superabsorbent anionic polyelectrolyte (the polymer in diapers). At low concentration in lubes it gives extreme water-swelling, stringy/threading texture, ultra-low kinetic friction. Characteristic of Japanese premium "ローション" (lotion) class lubes. Here it's doing all the slipperiness work alone, much like high-MW PEO does for 雅润.

## Comparison of the three slipperiness polymers

| Property | HEC (羟乙基纤维素) — Kiiroo | PEG-90M / PEO (聚乙二醇-90M / 聚环氧乙烷) — 雅润 | Sodium polyacrylate (聚丙烯酸钠) — Toysheart |
|---|---|---|---|
| Polymer class | nonionic cellulose ether (semisynthetic) | nonionic synthetic polyether | anionic synthetic polyelectrolyte |
| Backbone origin | cellulose + ethylene oxide modification | ring-opening of ethylene oxide | acrylic acid polymerized then neutralized to Na salt |
| Slipperiness mechanism | chain entanglement only | drag-reducing (Toms effect) + large extensional viscosity ("stringy") | charged side chains repel → chain fully extends + massive water swelling |
| Friction at sliding speed | moderate (smooth, not slick) | lowest among the three | very low, slick + stringy |
| Shear-thinning | mild | strong | strong |
| Effective concentration in lube | ~1–2 % | ~0.3–1 % (high MW means low w/w gives high viscosity) | ~0.1–0.5 % (superabsorbent) |
| pH sensitivity | none, stable pH 2–12 | none | needs pH > pKa ~4.5 to ionize and swell; loses viscosity if too acidic |
| Salt / ionic sensitivity | tolerant | tolerant | very sensitive — saline collapses the gel by charge screening |
| Tackiness as it dries | mild tackiness | film-forms, stays slick longer | crusts/films (superabsorbent gel) but slow to go tacky |
| Cost | cheap | moderate | cheap (diaper commodity) |
| Typical use beyond lube | eye drops, shampoo, pharma binder | medical lubricants (Surgilube, K-Y classic), drag-reduction tracers | diapers, soil hydrogels, paint thickener |

Summary axis: **HEC < PEO ≈ sodium polyacrylate** in raw slipperiness. PEO and sodium polyacrylate reach a similar "premium" class through orthogonal mechanisms (neutral drag-reducing chains vs charged extended polyelectrolyte), each with its own weakness — PEO is salt-tolerant but can shear-degrade; sodium polyacrylate stays intact under shear but collapses in salt water (relevant if mixed with sweat/body fluids in volume, less so at lube concentrations).
