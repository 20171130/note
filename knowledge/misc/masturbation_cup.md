## Heating

Henry specifically wants heating. Two approaches:

- **Built-in heating element** - automatic, thermostat-controlled, heats to ~37°C. Better experience, more expensive. Some automatic cups include this.
- **Separate USB heating rod** - inserted into the channel a few minutes before use. Cheaper, works with any manual cup. Risk: non-thermostatic rods can overheat and damage TPE - **always add lubricant before inserting rod**, and use thermostatic rods only.

**Recommendation:** A manual/suction cup + a thermostatic USB heating rod is the most cost-effective starting setup.

## Lubricant

**Always required** - dry use damages TPE channels.

| Type | Feel | Toy compatibility | Longevity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Water-based | Light, natural | ✅ All toys including silicone | Dries faster, needs reapplication | Safest, easiest cleanup |
| Silicone-based | Silky, slick | ❌ Degrades silicone toys; ✅ TPE, glass, metal | Very long-lasting | Good for TPE cups; avoid with silicone-material toys |
| Hyaluronic acid (HA) | Plush, cushioned, moisturizing | ✅ All toys | Longer than plain water-based | Water-based base + HA = natural moisture feel, fewer reapplications |

**Henry's question:** hyaluronic acid is indeed what creates the "different experience" - it provides a cushioned, skin-like glide that plain water-based lubes don't match. HA lubes are the upgrade from basic water-based without the cleanup hassle of silicone.

**Recommendation:** Start with a **HA-enhanced water-based lube** (e.g., Überlube Water, Sliquid H2O, Intimate Earth Soothe). Widely available on Amazon.

## Material Care (TPE)
- Rinse with warm water immediately after use.
- Wash with mild unscented soap or toy cleaner; dry **completely** - mold risk if stored wet.
- Apply cornstarch or renewal powder after drying to prevent stickiness.
- Store in a cool dry place, ideally in its original bag.
