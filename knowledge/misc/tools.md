- Corrosion resistance: LC200N, H1.



# WD-40
Water-displacing spray. Primary use: drive out moisture and leave a thin film that delays rust on bare-steel tools (carbon-steel knives, hinges, fasteners).
Not a long-term lubricant — the carrier evaporates within days; for sustained lubrication use a dedicated oil (mineral oil for food-contact blades, light machine oil for pivots).
Also frees stuck/seized parts and lifts light grime; do not use on rubber, painted surfaces, or anything that will contact food without wiping clean.

# Vaseline
Pure petroleum jelly: an occlusive — it does not moisturize, it seals in existing moisture and blocks evaporation.
- Skin: chapped lips/hands/heels, wound aftercare (clean first), minor burns, post-tattoo and post-procedure healing, barrier when applying hair dye near the hairline.
- Mechanical: long-term rust prevention on stored steel (lasts months, unlike WD-40), thread lubricant on bolts and battery terminals, frees stuck zippers/drawers/locks, conditions o-rings and most gaskets.
> Degrades latex (condoms, latex gloves, balloons). Compatible with nitrile, silicone, and most other rubbers.

# 3M tape
- Duct (cloth-backed): strongest general repair tape; bundling, patching, temporary fixes. Leaves residue if left for weeks.

Specialist:
- VHB: permanent mounting to smooth surfaces (glass, metal, finished plastic). Full bond after ~24-72 h.
- Electrical (Super 33+, vinyl): insulates and weatherproofs wire splices; stretches and conforms.
