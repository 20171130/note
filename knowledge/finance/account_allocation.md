# Account Allocation Strategy

*Related: [us_accounts](us_accounts.md) - step-by-step timeline for opening US accounts during the internship.*

**Core Principle:** Minimize friction and fees across the pipeline: Deposit → Convert → Hold/Earn → Withdraw → Spend.

## Recommended Setup
Keep 1-3 months' expenses (e.g., rent) in checking/spending accounts; move the rest to high-yield or investment accounts.
- **RBC / Chase:** Payroll destination and credit/debit spending. Keep minimum required balances.
- **Wise:** Multi-currency spending and small transfers.
- **IBKR (Pro):** Bulk savings, investing, and large FX conversions. One free withdrawal per month.

## 1. High-Yield Savings
Traditional banks (RBC/Chase) offer <0.5% (RBC cashier told me they offer 0.5% for saving account, much lower than inflation). Better alternatives:
- **IBKR (Pro):** ~4.5%+ on idle USD (tiering applies, first $10k earns 0%). Accrues daily, paid monthly.
- **Fidelity / Schwab:** ~4.5%+ (Money market / US only). Schwab is great for travel (no ATM/FX fees).
- **EQ Bank / Wealthsimple:** ~3-4% CAD savings.
- **Wise:** It showed 3.14% on USD, provided by its collaborator bank of montreal.

## 2. Currency Conversion (FX)
1. **IBKR Forex:** Interbank rate + ~$2 flat (or 0.002%). Best rate, but requires withdrawal step.
2. **Norbert's Gambit:** Cheapest CAD↔USD conversion via brokers (no spread, just commission).
3. **Wise / Revolut:** Mid-market rate + ~0.3-1.5% fee. Good for convenience. (Note: Bank of China beats Wise's spread in terms of exchange rate, although there is likely hidden fees).
4. **RBC / Chase:** 2-3% hidden spread. Avoid.
