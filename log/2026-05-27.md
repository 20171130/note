## 2026-05-27 12:25 → 12:55 PDT — 0526 run triage + nine fixes
Henry asked me to inspect runs `0526-{ctt,mse,highlr}` (he wrote "0525"; the

**Findings**
- `0526-mse` crashed in `evaluation.compute_physical_mae:515`:
  `np.asarray(pred_dict["lowdin_charges"], dtype=float64)` raised
  ValueError on ragged input. Per-record function — the shape gate assumed
  rectangular inputs, but partially-trained AR parsers emit ragged lists.
  Fix 1.
- `0526-mse` also showed 33/163 jsonl rows with NaN on
  `train/loss/cont_gauss/lowdin_charges`, 30 `non-finite grad` events, one
  auto codec-warmup→joint switch. Root cause: `data.py:585` defaults
  `lowdin_charges` to NaN-filled when the record lacks the QM analysis;
  Gaussian NLL propagates the NaN. Categorical (`Decoder`) bit-AR head
  survives because IEEE NaN has a fixed bit pattern. Fix 2: mask NaN target
  rows in the Gaussian scalar/vector NLL.
- `highlr` is materially better than `ctt`: at matched step 7900,
  integrated_densities -78.9%, text -39.1%, s_squared -22.1% on train loss.
  Henry cancelled ctt → Fix 3 dropped.

  sites). That's why even the healthy `highlr` was missing the side-by-side
  wandb.Html sample panel. Fix 6: wire it next to the sample-log site.
- MAE units: NOT a bug. Stats audit on
  `checkpoints/0526-highlr/latest/meta.json` shows raw `energy` has std
  127,800 eV (total electronic energy); a denormalized prediction MAE of
  ~5000 eV is then ~64,000 meV/atom — physically correct but misleadingly
  labelled. Fix 7: drop the `energy_*_per_atom` / kcal variants; keep
  `total_energy_eV` and the chemistry-relevant `atomization_energy_*`.
- omol25 LMDB shards each contain one ASE bookkeeping key `b'nextid'`
  (an int = next-available record id). `iter_shard` was iterating over it

  loss. Fix 4: skip non-digit keys at `iter_shard`.
- Pruned redundant sample keys (`parse_ok_{j}` — derivable from
  `parsed_json_{j}` presence; `n_atoms_{j}` — in parsed `numbers` and in
  the html ground truth). Demoted `eval/n_entries` from metric stream to
  stdout (general rule: bookkeeping → stdout, not metric dict). Fix 9.
- `shape_ok/<field>` is now emitted only for variable-length fields
  (forces, mulliken/lowdin charges); HOMO/s_squared/nl_energy/homo_lumo_gap

  Fix 8.

## 2026-05-27 12:50 → 13:06 PDT — alignment_shared vs lowest QOS
Jagriti recommended "alignment share cluster" for default experiments. It's
actually a QOS (`*_alignment_shared`), not a partition — on FAIR-SC scheduling
is by `--qos=`, not `--partition=` [fair-sc.md](../knowledge/fair-sc.md). Pulled live numbers from
`sacctmgr` + `squeue` on `hangrui-login-0`:

| QOS | Pri | Run | Cap | Pend | PD/RUN |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| h100_alignment_shared | 5 | 1420 | 1432 | 170 | 0.12 |
| h100_lowest | 1 | 74 | 2760 | 320 | 4.3 |
| h200_alignment_shared | 5 | 1348 | 3424 | 868 | 0.64 |
| h200_lowest | 1 | 8 | 7432 | 336 | 42 |

Counter-intuitive find: `*_lowest` has huge quota headroom but rarely runs.
Cluster-wide GPUs are fully consumed by `*_high` QOSes, so pri-1 jobs sit
indefinitely. Pri 5 (`alignment_shared`) is the lowest QOS that actually
gets scheduled.

**Verdict**: default to `*_alignment_shared`; `*_lowest` is a misnomer-trap
fallback. Folded the table into [fair-sc.md](../knowledge/fair-sc.md).

**Learning signal**
- *Instruction*: Jagriti's tip turned out correct — verified the mechanism
  rather than trusting blindly. Lesson: when a peer gives a cluster tip,
  measure once before adopting; here measurement strengthened the
  recommendation but could just as easily have refuted it.
- *Self-noticed*: First `squeue` parse silently dropped the gres value
  because `-o "%b"` packs gres into a single field with `gres/gpu=N` mixed
  with other TRES. Re-parsed with `-O "tres-alloc:200"` + regex. Lesson:
  when an aggregate sum looks like "0 across all rows", suspect the parse
  before the data.
