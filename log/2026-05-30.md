# CTT
It is very clear that CTT is better than MSE on every evaluation metric by a large margin, despite using per-field normalization.
However, one may argue MSE should be used together with parallel prediction, instead of autoregressive prediction.
It seems that the model converges after 200K steps, although the plateau may be due to LR scheduling.
The generated structures look tree-like, but often contain clashes.
Comparing train and eval loss, we find no sign of overfitting.
force CE going from 52 to 49.5, MAE goes from 300 to 117; MAE depends on CE exponentially. It is possible we can close the gap via scaling.
molxformer used a 1B model and trained for 10 pretrain + 60 finetune epochs; we only did about 4.
I removed mulliken_charges and lowdin_charges for fewer tokens per record and more efficient training.

`checkpoints/0527-ctt/latest/meta.json` (`tokens_seen=2.41B`).

**Eval accuracy** (both on OMol25 OOD-composition val; molxformer reports

mean_n_atoms by dividing by 50.5 for a magnitude-comparable column; CTT
reports per-atom natively):

| metric                       |  CTT  | molxformer | eSEN-sm-d[^esend] | eSEN-sm-cons[^esenc] |
|------------------------------|------:|-----------:|------------------:|---------------------:|
| atomization_energy (meV/atom)|  6.75 |  ~2.34     |       ~2.57       |          1.85        |
| forces (meV/Å)               | 116.9 | 18.35      |       13.01       |          7.37        |

→ CTT is **~2.9× worse than molxformer on energy** and **~6.4× worse on
forces**; molxformer itself sits between the eSEN-direct and eSEN-conserving
points (better than eSEN-d on energy, worse on forces).

**Training budget**:

| dimension              | CTT (0527-ctt)                        | molxformer                                  |
|------------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| backbone               | Qwen3-0.6B-Base                       | bespoke 1B Transformer                      |
| params                 | 0.6 B                                 | 1 B (~1.7×)                                 |
| train split            | OMol25 4M                             | OMol25 4M                                   |
| stages                 | **single AR stage**                   | 10ep AR pretrain + **60ep continuous FT**   |
| FT objective           | none (still next-token CE)            | direct continuous energy + force regression |
| AR epochs              | **~4.1 epochs**[^ctt_epochs]          | 10 epochs                                   |
| tokens through model   | ~19.3 B (8 ranks × 2.41 B/rank)       | ~10 B (10ep × 258 × 4M)[^molx_tokens]       |
| compute (rough 6·N·T)  | ~6.9e19 FLOPs (AR only)               | ~6.2e19 FLOPs (AR pretrain)[^molx_flops]    |
| eval head              | AR sampling + parse                   | dedicated continuous regression head        |

[^ctt_epochs]: per-rank `tokens_seen=2.41 B` at step 199k (meta.json) × 8 ranks
  ≈ 19.3 B total tokens through the model. Train-4M record token count
  mean = 1161 (p50=1032, p99=2714) per `scripts/ctx_stats.py` →
  `logs/ctx_stats_5726913.log`. 19.3 B / 1161 ≈ 16.6 M records / 4 M ≈ 4.1
  epochs.
[^molx_tokens]: From paper Figure 2 (p.3) schema: 5 tokens/atom (1 element +
  1 joint-xyz-position + 3 force) + ~5 fixed/record (BOS, ENERGY, EOS,
  charge/spin) → ~258 tokens/record at 50.5 atoms. Ratio to CTT's 1161
  tokens/record is **~4.5×** (Henry's < 5× expectation, 2026-05-30
  pushback), *not* the 20× I'd derived from the paper's "over 2B tokens"
  figure on p.6. That headline figure does not reconcile with the schema
  (10ep × 258 × 4M ≈ 10 B, not 2 B), so it must count only some subset
  (discrete-loss positions only?) — treating it as advisory only.
[^molx_flops]: Schema-derived: 6·N·T = 6 × 1e9 × 10ep × 258 × 4M ≈ 6.2e19
  for the pretrain stage. Paper's headline 8.5e19 FLOPs total (HuggingFace
  tooling) also does not reconcile with 70 total epochs at this schema
  density (~5e20 expected), suggesting their FLOP estimator either drops
  the FT-stage seq-len contribution or counts differently. Pretrain-only
  is the comparable column here regardless.

→ Once both per-record schema densities are accounted for, the **AR-pretrain
FLOPs are basically tied** (~6.9e19 vs ~6.2e19; CTT marginally larger because
its richer per-record token schema offsets its smaller backbone). The big
remaining budget delta is molxformer's **60-epoch continuous regression FT
stage** (energy + force heads, bidirectional attention) — CTT lacks this
entirely.

→ CTT's gap to molxformer on forces (6.4×) is much larger than on energy
(2.9×), matching the pattern that a regression head wins most when the
target is high-bandwidth and per-atom (forces, 3·N values per record).

This is consistent with the morning's "force CE 52→49.5 ↔ MAE 300→117" note —
MAE depends on CE exponentially, so closing even the ~3× model + token gap
could bring CTT into molxformer range, but the missing continuous-FT stage
will likely have to be added to reach eSEN-cons.
